    Solving Problems with Experience

    In the tech industry, experience isn't just about knowing syntax or frameworks—it's about understanding how to solve problems efficiently. Often, the ability to connect different technologies, apply logical reasoning, and make critical decisions under pressure makes a true expert. I approach every challenge by analyzing the core problem, breaking it into structured steps, and implementing a solution that is scalable, secure, and optimized.

    An example? Imagine debugging a critical production system failure. While some may jump into writing random fixes, I first analyze logs, replicate the issue, and identify root causes. This approach saves time and prevents unnecessary downtime. Experience isn't about remembering every function name—it's about knowing how to find the right answers quickly.

    A Perspective on Hiring & Interviews

    One of the biggest flaws in hiring processes today is focusing on testing memory rather than problem-solving skills. I have observed that less experienced interviewers sometimes ask questions that are either too theoretical or completely irrelevant to real-world problem-solving.

    Hiring someone should not be about how fast they can write a function on a live coding test—it should be about understanding how they approach complex challenges. The onboarding phase exists for a reason: to evaluate performance in a real environment, not under unrealistic test conditions.

    Experience should be respected and valued. A person with years in the industry knows how to solve problems, even if they don’t immediately recall the name of a specific algorithm. Problem-solving is about understanding concepts and applying knowledge, not reciting documentation.
